*Introduction*

Already applied in system 
*Waivers:* Relief based on a discretionary decision to "forgive" or "cancel" a debt (mostly interest or penalty) 
It is applied after liability is assessed but before payment.
Amount is no longer collectible

{*}Write-offs{*}: An accounting action to remove an uncollectible debt from active accounts (not the same as forgiveness).
It is applied after the debt is confirmed and collectible. 
Amount is legally due but no longer pursued. 

*Suppression:* 
A temporary hold or administrative decision to suppress or defer issuing an assessment , interest.
It is applied during the assessment/process. 
The due amount exists but it is not communicated or enforced yet.  

New :
*Exemption:* 
A lawful exclusion from a tax obligation defined in legislation or policy.
It is applied before a due amount/liability arises. 
(e.g. Charitable organizations are exempt from income tax.)
The tax {*}never becomes due{*}—it’s not assessed or recorded as payable.
