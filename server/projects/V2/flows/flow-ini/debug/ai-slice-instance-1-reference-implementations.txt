Issue Type: Feature | IRM: IRM-29513 | Roadmap Initiative Group: REFERENCE IMPLEMENTATIONS | Roadmap Initiative: APPLICATION PROCESSES | Roadmap Feature: Support four-eye principle in Solon Tax - Approval Workflow | Requester: GRC | PI: PI28 | Estimate: 42 | Feature Priority: p3 | Feature Status: Waiting Analysis Approval | Features Description: As an IRM Admin, I should be able to configure workflows, that enforce four-eyes approval within business processes, ensuring that one user initiates an action and a different user validates and approves it before completion.

*Introduction* 

This feature is a result of PSD-12702 for UBM project, however it is anticipated that all implementations may use this functionality to cover different business cases. As it is described in the related PSD: 

At present, SOLON does not support double attestation (four eye principle) out of the box in human case management, which conflicts with the following requirement specified in SKV's contract (applicable to UBM as well):

 
|​3.2.2.2.1.16|​Functional|​Case Management|​In the software, it shall be possible to configure the actions that require verification (double attestation) by two different case workers during manual handling, including at least:
 - Manual registration of debiting/crediting
 - Manual modification of a transaction on a tax account
 - Manual administration of Swedish and foreign disbursement accounts
 - Manual registration and modification of a disbursement block​|​LKVFM-2.9.306|

 

UBM need the possibility to employ double attestation on human tasks, such that a change affected by the completion of the case is verified by at least 2 distinct case workers. This is for example needed for imposing sanctions on either individual people, credit institutions, or countries, where one case worker would create the case, and another one would validate the right target is being sanctioned, approve the case, turn it back to the initiator, who could then complete the task and thereby enforce the sanction. A rejection should naturally not enforce anything, but require a change in the human task from the initiator, or closing of the case altogether. 

Dependent user stories for sanctions are attached as a PNG. They are not yet created as User Stories in toolkit. Furthermore manual registration of payment methods (e.g. for international bank accounts) require double attestation. It is expected that more functional cases that require double attestation will appear. As such, a generic approach to employing double attestation on a human task flow is needed.

A workaround is being worked on that could support double attestation, but is a process that requires a lot of manual setup (see attachment). If SOLON had the capability of staging a change for entities in a human task (without persisting it), waiting for either approval or rejection, the requirements for double attestation would be a simpler task to support.

> An admin must be able to configure whether one or multiple checkers are required before an item moves forward. 

> An admin must be able to set conditions (e.g., value thresholds, registration characteristics) that trigger additional approval steps. 

> An admin must be able to configure escalation paths if approvals are delayed beyond a defined time.

*Introduction*

This feature describes how the taxpayer can use the taxpayer self service portal to initiate a payment to pay outstanding liabilities.

The steps the taxpayer must follow to initiate and complete a payment may vary depending on the type of integration between ICARUS and the payment service providers (a.k.a. payment gateways or PSPs). Integration types to consider are (1) hosted - taxpayer leaves self service portal to complete payment on PSP portal, (2) direct post - PSP page that is used to complete the payment is embedded in a frame on self service portal and (3) non-hosted - self service portal provides features to capture all payment details and uses an API to submit details to the PSP. This feature describes the requirements assuming the 3rd type of integration is used.

Revenue authorities can depending on the requirements provide various options on the self service portal to pay outstanding liabilities, including for example (1) pay party role, (2) pay specific tax account, (3) pay a specific filing period, (4) pay a specific pay plan, (5) pay a specific collection notice, (6) pay a specific tax return, or (7) pay a specific single assessed liability (e.g. tax, penalty or interest). This feature describes the requirements for the 3rd and 7th option.

Revenue authorities can depending on the requirements provide various payment methods on the self service portal to pay outstanding liabilities. Examples include (1) credit card, (2) debit card, (3) PayPal and other similar services, (4) M-Pesa, Paym and other similar services, (5) direct debit, (6) PDS2 and other similar services and (7) guarantees registered at the revenue authority. This feature describes the requirements for the 1st option.

Revenue authorities and/or payment service providers may charge convenience fees. Information on any applicable fees must be presented to the portal user before submitting the payment. Convenience fee requirements will be documented in another feature.

As credit card details are used in the self service portal, the product should cater for features to allow the revenue authority to be PCI-DSS compliant. Relevant PCI-DSS requirements will be documented in another feature.

Some example wireframes have been attached to this feature. In case the content on the wireframes differs from the functional requirements, then the functional requirement should be used as input when implementing this feature.

*Functional Requirements*
 * User can see the details of a specific filing obligation for a specific taxpayer

 ** Actions:

 *** {color:#172b4d}User can open a new or draft amended tax return form for a filing obligation. Assumption is that the correct tax return form is opened for the specific tax type for which the overview is provided. Also, the assumption is that the tax return form can be prefilled with data that is already known - for example the taxpayer details, filing period details and data from the original tax return. User can only open a new or draft amended tax return if an original tax return has been submitted for this filing obligation. Assumption is that the user can only amend a tax return for specific tax types (e.g. for corporate income tax).{color}
 *** {color:#172b4d}User can open a payment form (see IRM-3542 for an example of a payment form) to pay the sum of all current positive balances of any payment obligations that are related to a specific filing obligation (e.g. late filing penalty or tax assessment and any related charges that were added after filing a tax return for this filing obligation). Assumption is that the payment form can be prefilled with data that is already known - for example the taxpayer details and the payment obligation details. User can only open a payment form if there is an outstanding amount that needs to be paid for these payment obligations.{color}
 *** {color:#172b4d}User can open a form to request a refund if an overpayment exists for one or more payment obligations that are related to a specific filing obligation (e.g. late filing penalty or tax assessment and any related charges that were added after filing a tax return for this filing obligation). Assumption is that the refund request form can be prefilled with data that is already known - for example the taxpayer details and the details of the payment obligations for which an overpayment exists. User can only open a refund request form if an overpayment amount exists for any of the payment obligations.{color}
 *** {color:#172b4d}User can open a form to request to offset an overpayment that exists for one or more payment obligations that are related to a specific filing obligation (e.g. late filing penalty or tax assessment and any related charges that were added after filing a tax return for this filing obligation). Assumption is that the request overpayment offset form can be prefilled with data that is already known - for example the taxpayer details and the overpayment amount that can be offset.{color}

 *** {color:#c1c7d0}User can upload a supporting document that can be used by the revenue authority to validate the content of the submitted tax return. User can only upload a document if the obligation to file a tax return has been met.
 *** User can open a financial overview (see IRM-3452) for the payment obligations that are related to a specific filing obligation (e.g. late filing penalty or tax assessment and any related charges that were added after filing a tax return for this filing obligation).
 * User can see what amounts have been assessed related to a specific filing obligation (e.g. late filing penalty or tax assessment that was added after filing a tax return for this filing obligation). Any charges (e.g. late payment penalty or interest) that are levied towards these original assessments are not considered in this overview.
 ** Data set - can contain data for multiple assessments
 *** Filing type: the type of submission that was done (e.g. submit preliminary, original or amended tax return)
 *** Letter reference number: unique reference number of the assessment notice in which the taxpayer was informed about the assessment
 *** Assessed amount: the amount that was assessed{color}

 
 * {color:#172b4d}User can see what draft tax returns exist for a specific filing obligation. These tax returns have not been submitted yet.{color}
 ** {color:#172b4d}Data set - can contain data for multiple draft tax returns - most often just one draft form{color}
 *** {color:#172b4d}Form type: the type of form that was saved as a draft{color}
 *** {color:#172b4d}Form reference number: the reference number of the form that was saved as a draft{color}
 *** {color:#172b4d}Last updated date: the date the draft form was last updated{color}
 *** {color:#172b4d}Status: the status of the form that was saved as a draft (e.g. saved){color}
 ** {color:#172b4d}Actions:{color}
 *** {color:#172b4d}User can open the draft tax return (see IRM-3539 for an example of a value added tax return form){color}
 * {color:#172b4d}User can see what supporting documents have been uploaded for a specific filing obligation.{color}
 ** {color:#172b4d}Data set - can contain data for multiple supporting documents{color}
 *** {color:#172b4d}Document type: the type of supporting document that was submitted to the revenue authority{color}
 *** {color:#172b4d}Document reference number: a unique reference number of the supporting document that was submitted to the revenue authority{color}
 *** {color:#172b4d}Submission date: the date the supporting document was submitted to the revenue authority{color}
 *** {color:#172b4d}Status: the status of submitting a supporting document (e.g. submitted){color}
 ** {color:#172b4d}Actions:{color}
 *** {color:#172b4d}User can open a supporting document{color}
 * {color:#172b4d}User can see what letters (e.g. reminder letter, notice letter) were sent to the taxpayer by the revenue authority.{color}
 ** {color:#172b4d}Data set:{color}
 *** {color:#172b4d}Letter type: the type of letter that was sent to the taxpayer{color}
 *** {color:#172b4d}Letter reference number: a unique reference for the letter that was sent to the taxpayer{color}
 *** {color:#172b4d}Description: a description of the letter that was sent to the taxpayer{color}
 *** {color:#172b4d}Letter date: the date the letter was considered to have been delivered to the taxpayer{color}
 ** {color:#172b4d}Actions:{color}
 *** {color:#172b4d}User can open a letter (see attached wireframe){color}
