*Introduction*

Already applied in system 
*Waivers:* Relief based on a discretionary decision to "forgive" or "cancel" a debt (mostly interest or penalty) 
It is applied after liability is assessed but before payment.
Amount is no longer collectible

{*}Write-offs{*}: An accounting action to remove an uncollectible debt from active accounts (not the same as forgiveness).
It is applied after the debt is confirmed and collectible. 
Amount is legally due but no longer pursued. 

*Suppression:* 
A temporary hold or administrative decision to suppress or defer issuing an assessment , interest.
It is applied during the assessment/process. 
The due amount exists but it is not communicated or enforced yet.  

New :
*Exemption:* 
A lawful exclusion from a tax obligation defined in legislation or policy.
It is applied before a due amount/liability arises. 
(e.g. Charitable organizations are exempt from income tax.)
The tax {*}never becomes due{*}—it’s not assessed or recorded as payable.

Other: 
[Assessment]->  Suppressions may be applied
[Billing & Payment] ->   Waivers may be issued
[Collection/ Enforcement]->  Write-offs recorded
[Audit & Compliance]-> for all

*Introduction* 

This analysis outlines the requirements for introducing a threshold amount field in the Revenue Type Registration entity to support Tax Account functionality in Solon Tax. The threshold field will enable implementation teams to configure automatic threshold determination rules while maintaining manual editability during the initial implementation phase. 

The threshold amount will be used in Tax Account scenarios where specific monetary limits determine processing behavior, validation rules, or reporting requirements. This field provides the foundation for threshold-based business logic while allowing implementation teams flexibility in defining automatic calculation rules.

*Functional Business Requirements*

Add a new field thresholdAmount to the Revenue Type Registration entity that:

> Stores a monetary threshold value for tax account processing
> Supports decimal precision for accurate financial calculations
> Allows null values (optional field)
> Is editable by users during creation and updates
> Can be automatically populated by system rules (future enhancement)



This enhancement provides the foundational threshold capability required for Tax Account implementations while maintaining the flexibility needed for diverse implementation requirements and future automatic rule integration.
